Multimodal Maritime Dataset for Unmanned Surface Vehicles in Challenging Circumstances

Jon Muhovič, Janez Perš
Faculty of Electical Engineering
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Example images from the nighttime test set. Semantic labels for sky, static obstacle, water and dynamic obstacle classes are shown in purple, green, blue and red, respectively

Abstract

Unmanned vehicles can encounter a number of varied visual circumstances, some of which can be very difficult to interpret. The variability of scene appearances in different weather and lighting situations can preclude the use of only visual spectrum cameras for scene interpretation. We present a novel multimodal maritime dataset MULTIAQUA (Multimodal Aquatic Dataset) that contains synchronized, calibrated and annotated data captured by multiple sensors of various modalities. The dataset is aimed at developing methods that can extract useful information from these modalities in order to provide a high quality of scene interpretation regardless of potentially poor visibility conditions. To illustrate the benefits of the proposed dataset, we present a multimodal semantic segmentation approach that enables reliable performance even in near-complete darkness. This approach allows for training a robust deep neural network only using daytime images, thus significantly simplifying both the annotation and the training process.
